About the Census

The Namibia Population and Housing Census is conducted every ten years to collect important demographic, social and economic data about all people in Namibia. The information gathered helps government and other organizations make informed decisions about resource allocation, policy development and service delivery.

Why Participate?

Census data helps determine how billions of dollars in federal funding flow into communities each year for schools, roads, hospitals, and other vital programs.

Confidentiality

By law, your census responses cannot be used against you by any government agency or court. Your answers are confidential and protected.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who should be counted in the census?

All persons who spent the census reference night (September 24, 2025) in this household including babies, old people, persons with disabilities, visitors and those who were on night shift (e.g., nurses, security guards, etc.) must be counted.

Is my information confidential?

Yes. The Statistics Act (Act no. 9 of 2011) requires that all information collected will be kept confidential and used only for statistical purposes. A document, information, or a record provided to the Agency that relates to an individual and household, may not be disclosed to a third party.

What if I don't participate?

The Statistics Act requires that all persons in Namibia participate in the census. People who misuse the data collected can be prosecuted.
